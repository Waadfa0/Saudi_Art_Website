Why Lavender ?


The Kingdom's plants and flowers are used in daily life: in food,
traditional crafts, such as the miswak and khoos, and even
in perfumes or drinks that are presented to guests upon arrival.
A symbol of Saudi identity and hospitality, lavender will now be
the color of ceremonial carpets that will be rolled out with pride
to welcome our guests to the Kingdom- a natural extension of
Saudi Arabia's hospitality.


Lavender also provides a splendid contrast with the forest green
color of our national flag, creating
a beautiful and welcoming spectacle
for guests to our country.

Colors and heritage

Some regions are famous for extracting and using certain
colors in textiles and engravings, including
Al-Qat Al-Asiri, Al-Sadu, and plaster inscriptions.

Blue

Extracted the color from white acacia and blue arsenic

Used in Alqatt Alasiri

Yellow

Extracted the color from safflower (Carthamus tinctorius) and flower petals.

Used in Alqatt Alasiri

Black

Extracted the color from a mixture of (stove charcoal) with black gum added

Used in Alqatt Alasiri and AlSadu

Red

Extracted the color from Hassan and Murr, carefully crushed fried rice.

Used in Alqatt Alasiri and AlSadu

Green

Extracted the color from white acacia and green arsenic.

Used in Alqatt Alasiri ,Plaster decorations and Our country's flag
